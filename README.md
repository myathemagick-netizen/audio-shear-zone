## How binaural beats work

Each ear receives a pure sine tone at a slightly different frequency. Because the two tones are delivered separately per ear (which is why **stereo headphones are required** — speakers cannot produce the effect), the brain does not hear two tones. It perceives a third, illusory tone whose frequency equals the difference between them.

For example: left ear 200 Hz + right ear 210 Hz = a perceived beat of **10 Hz**.

This illusory beat originates subcortically in the **medial nucleus of the superior olivary complex**, the first nucleus in the auditory pathway to receive input from both ears simultaneously. The internally generated frequency signal then propagates through the auditory cortex into broader neural networks. Through a mechanism called the **frequency-following response**, cortical oscillations tend to synchronize toward the difference frequency — this is brainwave entrainment.

A few perceptual thresholds worth knowing:

- Below ~3 Hz difference: the beat is perceived as a tone rotating from ear to ear
- 3–20 Hz: heard as a tone fluctuating in loudness — the primary working range
- Above 20 Hz: begins to sound rough; above 30 Hz the two tones are perceived as separate

## Carrier frequency

The carrier (base tone) shapes the timbre and comfort of the session. It also affects how clearly the brain perceives the beat. Gerald Oster's research (1973, *Scientific American*) produced what became known as the **Oster Curve** — a map of which carrier frequencies produce the most perceptible binaural beats at a given target frequency:

- Theta targets (4–8 Hz): carrier 160–210 Hz range is optimal
- Alpha targets (~10 Hz): carrier 230–240 Hz range is optimal
- General comfortable range: 200–500 Hz

Lower carriers (174–285 Hz) feel warmer and more grounding. Higher carriers (528–963 Hz) are brighter and more activating.

## Best practices

**Use stereo headphones.** This is non-negotiable. Earbuds work; over-ear are preferable for extended sessions. Speakers deliver both tones to both ears and cannot produce the binaural effect.

**Choose carrier and beat together.** The carrier shapes the feel; the beat frequency is the entrainment target. A 528 Hz carrier with a 7.83 Hz beat, for instance, places the grounding Schumann frequency on top of the "miracle tone" carrier — a deliberate attunement choice.

**For relaxation and trance work:** theta range (4–7 Hz), carrier 160–220 Hz. Sessions of 20–30 minutes are typical for reaching a stable theta state.

**For focus and cognition:** alpha-beta range (10–20 Hz), carrier 200–400 Hz.

**For deep sleep onset:** delta range (0.5–3 Hz). Not recommended for daytime use or tasks requiring attention.

**Add a noise bed.** Pink or brown noise behind the tones masks environmental interruption and adds a grounding texture. Pink noise has equal energy per octave and is gentler on long sessions; brown noise is deeper and more immersive.

**Ramp gradually.** The brain responds better to slow frequency transitions than sudden jumps. The Schumann Sweep feature demonstrates this — small continuous steps across a range are more effective than snapping between states.

**Set an intention before engaging.** The tool is a carrier of attention, not a replacement for it.

## Notes on the science

Research on binaural beats is real but mixed. EEG studies confirm that the brain does produce a frequency-following response to the difference tone. Whether this reliably induces the associated cognitive or consciousness states — and to what degree — remains an open question in the literature. The effect is genuine at the neurological level; the downstream subjective outcomes vary by individual, session length, and context.

The Solfeggio carrier frequencies are drawn from esoteric tradition. Their specific healing properties are not established in peer-reviewed science. They are included here as tools for intentional attunement within the Myathe Magick System framework, not as medical claims.
